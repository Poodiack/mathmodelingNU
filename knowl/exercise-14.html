Exercise 1.

A very simple assumption about the population of rabbits in a forest is that it grows at a rate proportional to the size of the population and the rabbits die at a rate proportional to the number of foxes in the forest. If \(p_n\) denotes the population of rabbits at time \(n\text{,}\) and \(F\) denotes the (constant) number of foxes, this assumption yields a model for the change in the rabbit population:

\begin{equation*} \Delta p_n = p_{n+1}-p_{n} = k_1p_n - k_2 F \end{equation*}

where \(k_1, k_2\ge 0\text{.}\)

(a)

Solve this model for \(p_{n+1}\text{.}\)

(b)

If \(p_0 = 500\text{,}\) \(k_1 = 0.15\text{,}\) and \(k_2 = 0.25\text{,}\) algebraically find the values of \(F\) for which the population of rabbits is decreasing, for which it is increasing, and for which it is constant for all \(n \ge 0\text{.}\) (Hint: If the population is constant, \(p_1 = p_0\text{.}\))

(c)

Use SageMath to numerically and graphically support your answer above.

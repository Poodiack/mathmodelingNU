We can compose two functions very easily in SageMath. Consider the two functions:

\begin{equation*} f(x) = 3x+5\quad\text{and}\quad g(x)=x^3 + 1. \end{equation*}

Perhaps we wish to explore \(f(g(x))\text{.}\) The easy way to do this is to define a third function, \(h(x) = f(g(x))\text{.}\) Then, we can print this function, and the values of \(h(x)\) whenever we like.

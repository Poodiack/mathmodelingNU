Proof

By definition, \(a\propto b\) and \(b\propto c\) mean that \(a=k_1 b\) and \(b=k_2 c\) for some nonzero constants \(k_1\) and \(k_2\text{.}\) So, substituting we get

\begin{equation*} a=k_1k_2c \end{equation*}

but \(k_1k_2\) is a nonzero constant, so \(a\propto c\) by definition.
